Research on Perovskites and Photoluminescence Metrology

I am a PhD student working with perovskites and developing a low-cost photoluminescence (PL) metrology system. My work aims to directly measure material properties such as quasi-Fermi level splitting and carrier lifetime, contributing to the broader field of renewable energy and materials science.

About Me

I am working on developing innovative solutions to advance perovskite-based materials. I am particularly interested in applications related to solar cells, optoelectronics, and sensing technologies. I also aim to contribute to the scientific community through accessible and impactful research tools, such as the development of my photoluminescence metrology system.

Recent Research

Perovskite Solar Cells

Exploring the properties of perovskites for high-efficiency solar cells and their impact on renewable energy.

Photoluminescence Metrology System

Developing a low-cost system to measure intrinsic material properties like carrier lifetime and quasi-Fermi level splitting in perovskites.

Material Characterization

Using advanced techniques to better understand the electrical and optical properties of perovskite materials under different conditions.
